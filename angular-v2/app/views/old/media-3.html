Light Painting Beyond the Shadows

Light painting to Beyond the Shadows. Find more great videos at http://noanchor.com/ A live-action light painting video we've been working on for quite some time. Set in an abandoned world, a mysterious figure searches to preserve the worlds light in any way possible. This video is No Anchor's first full length music video and our most intensive project so far! We used a Canon 60D to shoot RAW footage at a shorter aspect ratio with Magic Lantern. As for lenses, we exclusively used Canon's 50mm L-Series 1.2. We also used a GlidecamHD4000 for stabilization, and 2 thirty dollar LEDs for light. All the light painting lights were improvised from lights we had or created especially for this video. Audio Visual Animation - OFFICIAL NO ANCHOR SHORT

Our first official No Anchor audio visual short! We've wanted to release this one for a while! The computer head has been a prop of ours for a while. For this audio visual video we had one Canon 7D, a Glidecam, and our 50mm as well as our 20-35mm. Mike took Ed downtown Orlando and made him walking around with the computer head on with tracking points. Then, mike took the footage into after effects, cut it how he wanted and then tracked the computer head plane with Mocha AE which comes standard with after effects. Solid tracking points are needed to get a good track. Then mike made the shape animation on the display, loaded up the footage with the track info, and it was off to color correction. Thanks for watching and stay tuned for more No Anchor!

Super Slow Motion Parkour and Free Running with Twixtor [NO ANCHOR OFFICIAL]

Our first official No Anchor parkour and freerunning video! The story behind this one is great! Ed took a trip back home for his best friends wedding in september and they decided to rent all the video gear an extra day! For this slow motion parkour video we had two Canon 7Ds, a slider, Glidecam, and a Canon 28-70 2.8 L on top of our current gear.

Ed had been doing a lot of research on how to make Twixtor Pro for After Effects look good before leaving and it turned out great! Turns out shooting is just as important and the Twixtor settings you use in post. If you are trying to get a good slow motion shot with Twixtor be sure to shot at 60fps (or as fast as you can) with a shutter speed over 800. This will eliminate all motion blur and your shot will look very clean. Also be sure to shoot a dark subject over a bright background.
